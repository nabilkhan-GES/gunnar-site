THE CHALLENGE

Recovery economics hinge on the connection.

In-situ solution mining requires injection and recovery wells to be hydraulically connected in the target formation. If the wells don't connect where intended, recovery rates suffer and project economics fail.

Achieving and verifying that connection at depth is beyond the accuracy of conventional directional surveys, especially across a large well field.

THE GUNNAR APPROACH

Connect wells with deterministic accuracy.

Gunnar uses active magnetic ranging to intersect or precisely space wellbores for solution-mining cavern and well-field designs, ensuring the connections recovery depends on.

Our integrated ranging and directional capability holds tight tolerances repeatably across many wells — bringing predictable performance to mineral recovery.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Solution Mining — your questions, answered.

How does ranging help solution mining?

Solution mining requires injection and recovery wells to connect underground. Magnetic ranging deterministically intersects or precisely spaces wellbores so those connections form where intended — beyond the accuracy of conventional surveys.

Which minerals does this apply to?

Any in-situ solution-mining operation that depends on connected or precisely spaced wells — including potash, lithium and uranium recovery.

Can you control spacing across a large well field?

Yes. The same technology used to intersect wells holds precise, repeatable separation across many wellbores, enabling predictable well-field performance.

Is the connection verifiable?

Yes. Ranging produces distance-and-direction data that confirms the achieved geometry, supporting production and recovery planning.
