THE CHALLENGE

The hardest wells to abandon are the ones that matter most.

Many wells slated for abandonment have compromised integrity — collapsed casing, stuck tubing, or no usable surface access. Conventional methods rely on open-hole wireline runs that are slow, costly and carry well-control risk.

Old and unrecorded wells may have uncertain positions, and urban or offshore settings add access, environmental and regulatory pressure to every decision.

THE GUNNAR APPROACH

Intercept the well, seal it from the inside.

Gunnar uses active magnetic ranging to drill a relief-style path that intercepts the target casing precisely, then mills in and places a deep cement plug on the inside — guaranteeing a permanent, durable abandonment.

No-access ranging-while-drilling eliminates wireline round-trips and open-hole exposure, removing kick and blowout risk while dramatically cutting rig time and cost.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Plug & Abandonment — your questions, answered.

How does ranging reduce P&A cost?

No-access ranging-while-drilling eliminates time-consuming open-hole wireline round-trips and the associated well-control risk. On complex P&A this can save roughly half the rig time and millions of dollars versus conventional methods.

Can you abandon wells with no surface access?

Yes. That is the core advantage of ranging-based P&A: by drilling a new path that intercepts the target wellbore underground, Gunnar can seal wells that are inaccessible, collapsed or unrecorded from surface.

Do you handle offshore and urban P&A?

Yes. Compact systems like the 3.5" CTRWD™ are suited to rig-constrained, urban and offshore settings, and Gunnar manages the environmental and regulatory requirements that come with them.

Is the abandonment permanent and compliant?

Plugs are placed inside the target casing and the work is regulator-witnessed. Gunnar has delivered CalGEM-certified abandonments approved for site redevelopment.
